The Centovalli Railway (or "Hundred Valleys Railway") is a spectacular metre-gauge line that links Locarno, Switzerland, with Domodossola, Italy, via the scenic Valle Vigezzo and the rugged Centovalli region. First opened in 1923, the railway was designed to provide a direct route between the Lake Maggiore area and the Italian rail network while showcasing the region's natural beauty. With 83 bridges, countless tunnels, and dramatic elevation changes, the line is regarded as one of Europe's most picturesque train journeys. Unlike other more widely known Swiss Trains, such as the Glacier Express, the Centovalli is much more of a hidden gem, using shorter trains and carrying far fewer passengers. For more info, visit:
