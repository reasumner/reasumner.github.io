1904 Letter Exposes The Dark Horrors behind Custer's battle at Little Bighorn

Published at : 12 Dec 2025
In 1904, a dying secret was revealed. Theodore W. Golden, one of the last men to see General George Custer alive, broke his silence in a chilling personal letter. This is not a history book's account; this is a raw, first-hand testimony from a soldier who was there.
In this video, we unseal his letter to expose the dark horrors and critical mistakes behind the Battle of the Little Bighorn. Golden reveals Custer's altered behavior, the ignored warnings from scouts, the disastrous splitting of the command, and the brutal reality of Reno's retreat. He provides a shocking theory for Custer's reckless actions and describes the gruesome aftermath on the battlefield.
We dive deep into this rare primary source to bring you the untold story of Custer's Last Stand, straight from a 7th Cavalry survivor. Discover the truth that was hidden for decades.
