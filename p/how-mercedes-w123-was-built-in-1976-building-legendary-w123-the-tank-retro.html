When and where was the Mercedes-Benz W123 manufactured?
The Mercedes-Benz W123 was manufactured from 1976 to 1985 in Germany, primarily at the Mercedes-Benz factories in Stuttgart, Bremen, and Sindelfingen.

What made the Mercedes W123 unique, and why was it so popular?
The W123 became famous for its exceptional durability, reliability, and long lifespan. It was engineered with high-quality materials and tested to extreme standards, which made it a favorite among taxi drivers, businessmen, and families worldwide. Many W123s easily reached hundreds of thousands of kilometers, sometimes over a million.

What was the Mercedes-Benz W123 nicknamed, and what was special about its production?
The W123 was commonly nicknamed “The Tank” because of its strong construction and almost indestructible engineering. The production process was interesting because Mercedes used a combination of hand craftsmanship and precise mechanical assembly, focusing on quality over speed. Each car underwent thorough inspections to ensure long-term durability.
