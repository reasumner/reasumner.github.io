Quick Review of the Impressive Casio AQ-S820W Solar Powered Analog/Digital 100 Meter Watch

Published at : 13 Dec 2025
For a more in depth review of this watch, please see my other video: https://youtu.be/iBLDRq3SzPU

This watch was first released in May 2025, although Casio had previously released the AQS800 and AQS810, which look a bit different but have the same functions as this one. This new AQS820 was introduced in three different color configurations, but I think it would be a great candidate for even more variations in the years ahead. The older models use Casio's Module 5208 while the new watch, featured in this video, uses Module 5745. You can find manuals for those modules on Casio's website (or just watch this video and I'll tell you pretty-much everything that's in the manuals).

This watch isn't as tough as a G-Shock but still features 100 meters of water resistance (10 Bar). It has two analog hands plus an LCD window to display seconds along with many other functions. The watch has a dual-countdown timer with a maximum duration of 99 minutes and 55 seconds per timer and those timers can repeat up to 10 times. If you only want to use one timer and keep it simple, just set the other timer to 00:00.

The watch has 5 daily alarms plus an hourly signal or chime. It has a one-hour stopwatch. It has a World Time mode. It has an LED backlight for the digital display and a little bit of glow-in-the-dark material on the tips of the analog hands. And the most high-tech feature on this watch is Casio's Tough Solar technology. There's a solar cell built into the face of the watch to keep the battery charged when the watch is exposed to normal lighting on a regular basis (preferably every day). The watch also has a power save feature which turns off the LCD overnight. To take the watch out of that sleep mode just press any button or expose the watch to bright light. Also, the day of the week abbreviation in the default Timekeeping mode can be displayed in English, Spanish, Portuguese, French, German or Italian.

The AQS820 has a suggested retail price of $55.95 and is readily available (as of the day this video was made) at both Casio's website and at Amazon's website for that price. You can also find the AQS800 and AQS810 for $55.95 from Casio or you can shop around and find them for a lower price elsewhere.
