Woman Builds Off-Grid Wooden House on an ISLAND in 120 Days | Start to Finish @LyThiHoa1987

Published at : 12 Dec 2025
On a remote island, one woman builds an entire world with her own hands. From felling trees to crafting beams, from engineering a bamboo bridge to reshaping a hillside into terraces, paths, and living spaces, she transforms raw wilderness into a fully functioning off-grid homestead.

Every structure is raised through ingenuity rather than machinery, using local timber, bamboo, stone, and earth. What begins as a simple wooden shelter grows into a breathtaking, multi-level sanctuary suspended above water and forest, complete with a handcrafted stove, natural stone walkways, and furniture carved from the same trees that surround her.

This is project is a big one, a one-woman feat of architecture, engineering, and survival artistry.

Keep with us to witness an inspiring journey where patience, creativity, and the rhythm of nature shape a home unlike any other.
