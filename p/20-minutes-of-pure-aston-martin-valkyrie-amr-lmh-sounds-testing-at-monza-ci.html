20 minutes of pure Aston Martin Valkyrie AMR-LMH sounds testing at Monza Circuit!

Published at : 13 Dec 2025
Back to last May when The Heart of Racing Team was at Monza Circuit with their Aston Martin Valkyrie AMR-LMH for a 2-days test ahead of the 24 Hours of Le Mans. I've already uploaded a couple of videos from that test but I hadn't yet shared with you the non-binaural/standard stereo video fully focused on the British brand's hypercar.

Aston Martin was one of the first manufacturers to commit to the Le Mans Hypercar class in 2019, with plans to start competition with at least two factory cars in the 2021 FIA World Endurance Championship. However the initial project was scrapped in February 2020, following the ACO's decision to allow the more cost-effective LMDh vehicles to participate in the Hypercar Class project, as well as Aston Martin's entrance into Formula 1, which left the company with limited finances to support the programme. The project was revived three years later in 2023 with the plan to enter at least one car in both the FIA World Endurance Championship and the IMSA SportsCar Championship in 2025, run by factory team The Heart of Racing, allowing them to be the first Le Mans Hypercar manufacturer to enter the IMSA series, which up to that point only had LMDh cars participate.

Named the Valkyrie AMR-LMH, the car is a heavily modified racing version of the track-only Aston Martin Valkyrie AMR Pro fitted to Le Mans Hypercar regulations, making it the only car in its class to be derived from a preexisting production car. It uses the same 6.5-litre Cosworth RA V12 engine of the production versions, but with lot of changes to run leaner in order to both meet the Le Mans Hypercar class performance window and increase reliability. The V12 is linked to a Xtrac 7-speed sequential gearbox. Despite not using a hybrid system, the car is able to launch from a standstill without requiring combustion engine power, using a combined starter and alternator motor system that Aston Martin calls a 'starternator' and which has been kept from the AMR Pro. The AMR-LMH was rolled out in the summer of 2024, ahead of an extensive testing program and made its global racing debut at the 2025 Qatar 1812 km, first round of the 2025 WEC season.

Regarding the actual footage from this test, I have to say that it didn't turn out to be the best of circumstances, despite the excellent opportunity to record the sound of the V12 in complete tranquility. Starting from the fact that the car was equipped with silencers, the wind set the terms: if in some corners it was a real pleasure to hear the LMH getting closer, in others, with headwinds, the low noise level was highlighted a lot. Anyway it's really nice to still have such a great sounding car in endurance racing nowadays!
