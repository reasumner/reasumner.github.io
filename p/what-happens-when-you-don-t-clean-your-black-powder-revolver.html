What Happens When You Don't Clean Your Black Powder Revolver

Published at : 12 Dec 2025
Replicas of civil war era black powder revolvers are very common today and most are made in Italy. I found this used Remington 1858 in 44 caliber made by Pietta at a tag sale. I wasn't able to pull back on the hammer to test it's function, however it was cheap enough that I picked it up anyway.
What follows is a lesson learned - why you should clean your black powder revolver after shooting it. Because this one hadn't been cleaned after the last time it was shot, and even though the outside appearance didn't look too bad, the internals were completely seized up. The hammer I already knew about, but the cylinder and cylinder pin were rusted solid as well.
