How The ENGINE Of The Worlds FASTEST Passenger Jet is Made

Picture a metal cylinder the size of a London double-decker bus, spinning fast enough to gulp down more than a ton of air every second. Now, attach four of them to the wings of a jet that carries an entire small town at nine hundred kilometers per hour. Welcome to the Rolls-Royce Trent 900, one of two engines that power the Airbus A380. Today, you will see how engineers turn raw titanium, nickel super-alloys, and space-age ceramics into eighty thousand pounds of pure thrust.
