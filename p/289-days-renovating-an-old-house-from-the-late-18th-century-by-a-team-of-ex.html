289 days. Renovating an old house: From the late 18th century: By a team of experts.@carlroge

Published at : 12 Dec 2025
This team of experts possesses a solid foundation in 18th-century architectural history, understanding every detail such as traditional timber structures, mortise and tenon joinery techniques, roof styles, patterns, and original materials. As a result, the renovation process always preserves the house's antique charm.

The experts are highly skilled in constructing and restoring antique details: recreating columns, beams, wooden ceilings, and characteristic motifs using traditional methods combined with modern machinery for maximum precision.

The renovation team adheres to the motto "preserving the soul ‚Äì renewing what is necessary." Deteriorated structural elements are reinforced with durable materials, while areas of historical significance are meticulously preserved to maintain their historical value.

They selected materials close to their original age, such as teak, stone blocks, and handcrafted fired bricks, while applying modern techniques to prevent termite infestation, mold, and wood damage, thus extending the structure's lifespan.

The team meticulously assessed the current condition before restoration. This ensured that every detail was precisely calculated as originally intended.

They worked carefully to avoid damaging the ancient architectural elements. Each construction step was continuously monitored to ensure accurate proportions, colors, and authenticity.

The team not only repaired but also restored the soul of the house: the light, space, atmosphere, and cultural story of the early 18th century. The finished structure possesses both classic beauty and meets living needs.
