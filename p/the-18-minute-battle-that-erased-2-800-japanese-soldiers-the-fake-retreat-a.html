The 18-Minute Battle That Erased 2,800 Japanese Soldiers — The Fake Retreat at Saipan...

Published at : 12 Dec 2025
It lasted just 18 minutes — but in that short span, over 2,800 Japanese soldiers were erased from existence. The Battle of Saipan was already a bloodbath, but this moment — known by historians as the Fake Retreat — was one of the most devastating traps in Pacific War history.

After days of brutal fighting, American Marines appeared to fall back under heavy fire, leaving behind weapons, vehicles, and wounded men. The Japanese forces believed victory was at hand… until the trap was sprung. Hidden artillery, machine guns, and tanks opened fire in perfect unison, cutting through entire formations in seconds. The counterattack was so swift and precise that survivors called it “hell on earth.”

• How this single moment broke Japan’s defense on Saipan
• Why it became one of the most haunting victories of WWII
