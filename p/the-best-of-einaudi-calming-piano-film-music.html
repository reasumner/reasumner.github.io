About the composer:
Ludovico Einaudi is an Italian pianist and composer, born on November 23, 1955, in Turin. He studied at the Conservatorio Verdi in Milan. Einaudi began gaining international recognition in the 1990s with albums like Le Onde and I Giorni, which showcased his distinctive minimalist style. Blending classical music with elements of pop, ambient, and world music, his compositions are known for their emotional depth and simplicity. His work has been featured in numerous films and TV shows, including The Intouchables and Nomadland. In addition to music, Einaudi is an advocate for environmental issues and famously performed on a floating platform in the Arctic to raise awareness about climate change. His calm, introspective pieces continue to resonate with a global audience, making him one of the most-streamed classical artists of the 21st century.
