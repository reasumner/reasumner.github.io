Published at : 14 Dec 2025
The blue crane, also known as the Stanley crane, is a stunningly beautiful bird that is found in southern Africa. It is the national bird of South Africa and is known for its distinctive blue-gray plumage, long legs, and elegant posture.

The blue crane is a large bird, standing up to 4 feet tall, with a wingspan of up to 6 feet. It is primarily blue-gray in color, with a white face and long, slender legs that are colored black. It has a long neck and a small head, with a red patch of skin on the crown.

Blue cranes are known for their graceful movements, which include a slow, sweeping dance that is often performed during mating rituals. They are also known for their distinctive trumpeting calls, which can be heard from a great distance.

Unfortunately, the blue crane is listed as vulnerable on the IUCN Red List due to habitat loss, hunting, and other threats. Conservation efforts are underway to protect this magnificent bird and its habitat, but more work is needed to ensure its long-term survival.
