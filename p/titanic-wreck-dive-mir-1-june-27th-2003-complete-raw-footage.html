Titanic Wreck Dive | MIR-1 June 27th, 2003 | Complete Raw Footage

Published at : 13 Dec 2025
On June 27th, 2003, a tandem dive was conducted by DSV's MIR-1 and MIR-2 at the wreck site of the RMS Titanic. For nearly 6 hours, the two submersibles worked their way between both major sections of the wreck and scouring portions of the central debris field. This video is a continuous feed from the outboard camera on MIR-1 showing the entire dive operation from start to finish.

During this dive, MIR-1 spent approximately 4 hours surveying the stern section and making observations of IPSCO material decay test platforms which had been left at the site on previous expeditions before crossing over the central debris field to continue observations of additional platforms on and around the bow section.

I had previously released this in 30 minute segments from the original Betamax tapes via the Titanic Archive Project, this release has combined the footage set to better represent the experience of participating in this dive.
