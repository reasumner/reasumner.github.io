Overview

Long the poster child for practical transportation, the Toyota Corolla's virtues aren't measured in thrills but instead in efficiency and value. Toyota has been leveraging that name recognition lately, spinning off first the Corolla Cross pseudo-SUV and recently the limited-edition GR Corolla, both reviewed separately. The Corolla sedan and hatchback models remain the bread and butter of the lineup, and both use a trusty but unhurried 169-horsepower inline-four and automatic transmission. While performance maybe lackadaisical, the Corolla has a predictable and unfussy nature. What's New for 2025?

2025 brings the Corolla FX, essentially a cosmetic package applied to the four-door sedan that leans heavily on the black it out and add a spoiler school of design. It attempts to pay tribute to the Corolla FX16 from the 80s but sadly shares few of the original's performance enhancements. Shorter springs lower the car slightly, and re-tuned electric power steering is said to offer a sportier feel. Cosmetics include 18-inch satin-black alloy wheels with machined accents, a blacked-out roof, a rear spoiler and black badging and mirror caps. The FX also marks the arrival of a new 10.5-inch infotainment screen as standard equipment; it will be an option on the rest of the 2025 Corolla lineup.

The Corolla SE sedan comes standard with plenty of features to keep us satisfied, including LED headlights, 18-inch wheels and tires, a leather-trimmed steering wheel, and an 8.0-inch touchscreen infotainment system that's compatible with Apple CarPlay and Android Auto.
