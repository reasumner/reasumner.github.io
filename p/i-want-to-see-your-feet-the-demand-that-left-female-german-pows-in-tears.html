In the final months of World War Two, German women taken into Allied custody expected interrogation, punishment, and humiliation.

Instead, on their very first day inside captivity, they were given one strange and unsettling command that left many of them confused and frightened.

Show me your feet.

In this video, we explore the real historical reason behind this unexpected demand, how wartime disease threatened entire POW and internment camps, and why Allied doctors performed emergency medical inspections on prisoners the moment they arrived.

What seemed at first like humiliation was actually a life saving medical procedure designed to stop the spread of trench foot, frostbite, and dangerous infections.

This is a true World War Two story about captivity, survival, wartime medicine, and the shocking reality faced by German women at the end of the war. It reveals how misunderstanding, and medical necessity collided inside overcrowded camps in 1944 and 1945.
