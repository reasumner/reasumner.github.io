How Americans Proved Rommel Wrong After Kasserine Pass

Published at : 14 Dec 2025
Discover the incredible true story of how the United States Army transformed from defeat to victory in just thirty days during World War Two. After the devastating loss at Kasserine Pass in February nineteen forty three, where American forces suffered over six thousand casualties and lost nearly two hundred tanks, the army faced its darkest hour in North Africa. General George Patton took command and implemented sweeping changes that would reshape the entire force. At the Battle of El Guettar on March twenty third nineteen forty three, the six zero one Tank Destroyer Battalion and the First Infantry Division faced down the veteran German Tenth Panzer Division in what became the first significant American victory against German armor. This documentary explores the leadership failures that led to Kasserine, the dramatic reforms under Patton, the heroic stand of American tank destroyers against overwhelming odds, and the daring twelve mile night march by Darby's Rangers through impossible terrain. Featuring the stories of real soldiers like Sergeant John Nowak and commanders like Terry Allen and Theodore Roosevelt Junior, this video reveals how an inexperienced army learned to fight and win against one of history's most formidable military forces. The lessons learned in the deserts of Tunisia would carry American soldiers from North Africa to Sicily, Italy, Normandy, and ultimately to victory in Europe. Watch to learn how disaster became the foundation for triumph and how the army that fled at Kasserine became the army that liberated a continent.
