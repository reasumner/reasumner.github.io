3 WORST and 4 BEST Mercedes Engines to Own
Discover the ultimate guide to Mercedes-Benz engines before you buy your next luxury vehicle! In this comprehensive breakdown, I reveal the 3 WORST and 4 BEST Mercedes engines based on real-world reliability data, repair records, and expert mechanic insights.

The worst offenders will drain your wallet faster than Vegas on payday. Learn why the M102's timing chain issues can lead to catastrophic $6,000 engine rebuilds, how the OM651 diesel's rear-mounted timing chain requires removing the entire engine for repairs, and why mechanics call the OM642 V6 diesel "the retirement fund" due to its expensive injector and oil cooler problems.

But it's not all bad news! Mercedes has produced some truly legendary powerplants. The nearly indestructible OM606 diesel routinely hits 500,000+ miles with basic maintenance, while the M113 V8 combines modern performance with old-school reliability. Discover why the hand-built M156 AMG V8 is becoming a collector's item, and how the simple M111 four-cylinder can outlast most modern engines.

Whether you're shopping for a used Mercedes or wondering if your current Benz is a keeper or a lemon, this video provides the critical information you need to make informed decisions and potentially save thousands in repair costs.

I break down exactly what goes wrong with each problematic engine, how much repairs typically cost, and what to look for when buying. For the reliable engines, learn what maintenance is required to keep them running for hundreds of thousands of miles.
