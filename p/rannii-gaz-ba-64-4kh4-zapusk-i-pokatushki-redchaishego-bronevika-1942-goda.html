The BA-64 armored car was developed in the fall of 1941, based on the GAZ-64 light off-road vehicle. This compact armored vehicle featured small arms protection and a two-seater cabin. Production of this small armored car began in the spring of 1942. The first version of the BA-64 had a narrow 1240 mm track, 4x4 all-wheel drive, and a 3.3-liter gasoline engine producing 50 hp. Almost no such vehicles remain in their original condition. This is a rare armored car from the USSR, featuring original components and assemblies that match the 1942 production year. Despite its age, I was able to start the BA-64 and take it for a test drive. Today, the armored car is housed in the "Motors of War" Museum in Moscow, where I captured it on video.
